# Lab Reflection - Week 1

## What I Learned
During this first week of the ENGCE301 course, I focused on the fundamentals of Software Engineering and setting up a professional development environment.

### Key Takeaways:
1.  **Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC):** I gained a better understanding of how software goes from a concept to a finished product through planned phases.
2.  **Agile & DevOps:** Learning about iterative development and the synergy between development and operations was highly insightful.
3.  **Core Web Technologies:** Implementing the "Hello World" workshop refreshed my skills in semantic HTML, modern CSS (using glassmorphism), and interactive JavaScript.
4.  **Version Control:** Setting up Git and understanding GitHub's role in collaborative development is essential for a software engineer.

### Challenges Faced:
- Designing a "premium" feel for a simple Hello World page required careful attention to typography, color palettes, and subtle animations.
- Understanding the balance between complex design and clean, maintainable code.

## Future Goals
I aim to dive deeper into system modeling and requirements analysis in the coming weeks, as these form the blueprint for any robust software system.
